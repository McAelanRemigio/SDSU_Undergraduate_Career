## Scale and Impact

- SSA maintained a historical membership base of **180+ students**
- Typical meeting attendance ranged from:
  - ~25 students during periods of stability
  - ~15 students during rebuilding efforts
  - Fewer than 10 students during later semesters as departmental cohorts shifted

Despite declining attendance, SSA programming continued to provide:
- Direct exposure to industry and academic career paths
- Niche insight into actuarial science and applied statistics
- A professional bridge between coursework and real-world expectations

---

## Leadership Experience and Growth

Leading SSA differed significantly from founding DSI. Rather than creating new systems, I worked within an existing organization, learning how leadership functions under constraint.

Key differences included:
- Shadowing experienced officers before stepping into executive responsibility
- Experimenting with leadership styles in a lower-risk environment
- Navigating institutional inertia and limited recruitment pipelines

This experience helped clarify what kind of leader I wanted to be and highlighted the importance of structure, clarity, and adaptability.

---

## How SSA Influenced What Came Next

My time in SSA directly informed the creation of the **Data Science Initiative (DSI)**. While SSA served students in applied statistics and actuarial science, I saw a growing population of data science students without a comparable home.

DSI emerged as a complementary successor focused on data science identity, applied learning, and opportunity-building, and was shaped by lessons I had learned while leading SSA.
